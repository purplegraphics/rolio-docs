---
title: "Billing FAQ"
description: "Common questions about Rolio billing and subscriptions."
---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I change plans at any time?">
    Yes. You can upgrade or downgrade at any time from the Billing page. 
    Upgrades take effect immediately. Downgrades take effect at the end of your current billing period.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens when I cancel?">
    When you cancel, your subscription continues until the end of your current billing period. 
    After that, your account moves to the free tier. Your data is preserved for 90 days, 
    then permanently deleted. Export your data anytime before deletion.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do you offer refunds?">
    We offer a 30-day free trial on all plans. After the trial, subscriptions are 
    non-refundable except as required by applicable law.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if I exceed my member limit?">
    You can view and manage existing members, but cannot add new members until 
    you either upgrade your plan or reduce your member count below the limit.
    Existing member records are never automatically deleted.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is there a discount for nonprofits?">
    We don't currently offer nonprofit-specific discounts, but our pricing is 
    designed to be accessible for small organizations. Contact support@getrolio.app 
    to discuss your situation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does annual billing work?">
    Annual billing is charged upfront for 12 months at a 20% discount. 
    Your subscription renews automatically each year unless cancelled.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I add the AI Power Bundle to any plan?">
    Yes. The AI Power Bundle ($29/month) can be added to any plan — Trial, Starter, Pro, or Organization.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who manages billing?">
    Billing is handled securely by Stripe. Click Manage subscription on the Billing 
    page to update payment methods, download invoices, or cancel.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
